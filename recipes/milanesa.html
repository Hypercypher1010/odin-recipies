Milanesa

Description

Milanesa is a popular dish in many Latin American countries, particularly in Argentina, Uruguay, Paraguay, and Venezuela. It is typically made with thinly sliced meat, usually beef, which is breaded and fried until crispy. The breading can be made from bread crumbs, flour, or a mixture of both, and sometimes includes seasonings such as garlic, paprika, or oregano.

To prepare milanesa, the meat is first pounded thin to tenderize it and ensure even cooking. It is then dipped in beaten eggs, coated in the breading mixture, and fried in hot oil until golden brown. Once cooked, the milanesa can be served hot with a variety of accompaniments, such as mashed potatoes, salad, or a side of fries.

In some variations of milanesa, the beef is replaced with chicken, pork, or even soy protein for a vegetarian option. There are also different regional variations, such as the Milanesa Napolitana, which includes tomato sauce and melted cheese on top of the breaded meat.

Milanesa is a beloved comfort food in many Latin American households and can be found in a variety of settings, from home-cooked meals to casual restaurants and street vendors.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs (900g) beef round, thinly sliced ​​(1 cm thick/ 1/2-inch thick)
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 2 cups (250g) breadcrumbs
  • 2 garlic cloves, chopped
  • 3 tbsp parsley, chopped
  • salt
  • black pepper, ground
  • vegetable oil (for frying)

Preparation

  1. Slice the beef into 1 cm (1/2-inch) thick cutlets.
  2. Cut off all the fat and the sinew to get as clean of as cutlets as possible.
  3. Tenderize the cutlets with a mallet until half a centimeter (1/5-inch) thick.
  4. Whisk the eggs with chopped garlic, chopped parsley, salt, and pepper.
  5. Drench the cutlets in the egg mixture, then let them soak in it in the refrigerator for 45 minutes.
  6. Heat the oil over medium-high heat until it reaches 170°C/340°F.
  7. Coat the cutlets in breadcrumbs and fry them on both sides for a few minutes, then place them on paper towels to drain.
  8. Serve warm, with some of the following: French fries, mashed potatoes, green salad, potato salad, or roasted peppers.
